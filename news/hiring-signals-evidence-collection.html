When job posts mention “evidence collection”, the pain is already real

Hiring often reveals what teams won’t say publicly: SOC 2 readiness work has become a recurring operational cost.

Updated: 2026‑03‑14 · Reading time: ~4 min

The signal

A job description is a budget decision. When a company explicitly asks for “SOC 2 readiness” and “evidence collection”, it usually means one of two things: (1) a major enterprise deal is blocked by security review, or (2) the team is approaching an audit deadline and the manual evidence chase has become unmanageable.

Why evidence work becomes expensive

  • Evidence is scattered: Drive screenshots, Slack decisions, email approvals.
  • Ownership is unclear: “who signs off” changes by situation.
  • Time windows drift: evidence exists, but not for the audited period.
  • Cross‑system mapping is missing: Jira tickets don’t connect to GitHub changes.

What auditors / procurement actually want

  • Clear scope: which systems and teams are in‑scope.
  • Traceability: timestamps + approval trail.
  • Repeatability: the same question should produce the same evidence next month.

This is why “policy PDFs” alone don’t close reviews. Proof needs an evidence chain.

EvidenceOps commentary

Hiring is a lagging indicator. The better fix is to operationalize evidence: define owners, approvals, and where audit logs live. In practice, the fastest start is to pick a small, evidence‑friendly scope and ship a pack your auditor can read.
