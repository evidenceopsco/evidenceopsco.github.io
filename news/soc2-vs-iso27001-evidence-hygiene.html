SOC 2 vs ISO 27001: frameworks differ, evidence hygiene doesn’t

Frameworks change vocabulary. Auditors still ask for the same fundamentals: scope, timestamps, owners, approvals, and traceability.

Updated: 2026‑03‑14 · Reading time: ~4 min

Why teams run both

ISO 27001 often provides an internal management backbone (ISMS). SOC 2 is commonly used as customer-facing assurance in B2B sales. In practice, teams adopt both because customers ask for different “proof formats”.

The trap: duplicating evidence work

  • Separate spreadsheets per framework.
  • Different naming conventions per team.
  • Evidence stored in multiple locations without a source of truth.

The result: every audit becomes a re-build, not a re-run.

The fix: framework-agnostic evidence hygiene

  • One evidence ID per artifact with timestamps.
  • One owner per control area.
  • One approved chain: ticket → approval → change/config → audit log.
  • One index that maps evidence to whichever framework you need.

EvidenceOps commentary

You don’t need two separate “compliance engines”. You need one evidence pipeline, and two views: ISO controls and SOC 2 controls. The pipeline is the expensive part — make it repeatable.
