SOC 2 announcements are increasing — continuous evidence is the hard part

SOC 2 milestones help sales. Maintaining evidence across systems is what makes teams burn out.

Updated: 2026‑03‑14 · Reading time: ~4 min

What announcements signal

When companies publicly announce SOC 2 Type I/II, it usually reflects repeated customer pressure: procurement wants assurance, fast. Public milestones reduce friction — but they also turn audits into an ongoing business function.

Where the cost hides

  • Ownership: who signs off on each control every month/quarter.
  • Timestamps: evidence must match the audited period.
  • Approvals: changes without an approval trail become findings.
  • Retention: logs exist, but are not retained long enough.

Why “evidence ops” is different from “docs”

Documents describe intent. Evidence shows execution. For audits and enterprise buyers, execution wins.

  • Policies without tickets/logs/approvals are weak.
  • Evidence without indexing and scope is unusable.
  • Evidence without repeatability becomes a quarterly fire drill.

EvidenceOps commentary

A credible SOC 2 story is not “we passed once”. It’s “we can reproduce proof on demand”. That requires a workflow: collection, review, approval gates, and a living gap backlog.
