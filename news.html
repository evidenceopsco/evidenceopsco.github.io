In the news

A lightweight digest of compliance/trust signals — plus our commentary on why evidence operations matters.

Trust signal

Companies publicly announcing SOC 2 milestones

More Taiwan teams now publish SOC 2 Type I/II updates as part of sales enablement.

  • Example: Keypasco SOC 2 Type II announcement: public post
  • EvidenceOps take: after the announcement, the hard part is continuous evidence maintenance (who approves, where logs live, what gets sampled).
Market pressure

Security questionnaires are becoming routine

Even before formal audits, procurement requests repeatable proof (not just policy PDFs).

  • EvidenceOps take: the fastest path isn’t “write more docs” — it’s building a short evidence chain: ticket → approval → config → audit log.
  • What we ship in the 14‑day PoV: an evidence pack sample + a gap backlog you can assign.
Operational reality

SOC 2 vs ISO 27001 (and why teams run both)

Many orgs use ISO 27001 as an ISMS backbone and SOC 2 for customer-facing assurance.

  • Reference: EY explainer: SOC 2 vs ISO 27001
  • EvidenceOps take: frameworks differ, but evidence hygiene is the same — names, timestamps, scope, owners, and approvals.
Proof pattern

Trust Centers are “evidence packaging” at scale

More vendors maintain public Trust Centers to reduce sales friction.

  • Example: Trend Micro Trust Center compliance page: public page
  • EvidenceOps take: Trust Centers work when the internal evidence pipeline is repeatable and reviewable.
Signal from hiring

SOC 2 readiness appears in job descriptions

When roles explicitly mention “evidence collection”, it’s usually already painful.

  • Example job post (104): SOC 2 readiness / evidence collection
  • EvidenceOps take: we can turn the “tribal knowledge” into a controlled checklist + pack you can hand to an auditor.
